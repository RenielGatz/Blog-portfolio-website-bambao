Demo-Teaching

Reflection 1

On day 1 of demo teaching (week 5), I was fascinated to learn about home economics and pursue one of the most interesting topics - proportional and conversion unit (mensuration and calculation). I chose to teach about the caregiving field because some of the presenters (demo-teachers) were teaching about tools and equipment. I wanted to challenge myself to teach a topic that wasn't in my field. It was hard, but it taught me a lot about the importance of lesson planning and analyzing the presentation.

During that moment, I thought about creating a website that has valuable information about home economics, like an e-portfolio, to help those teachers who struggle to search for information about home economics. I started to create a scratch website with databases and drafted a portfolio. However, in some instances, I realized that it might be time-consuming. To be honest, I wanted to create valuable online resources, but we all face personal problems and challenges in life.

Actually, during the demo-teacher, one thing that I took away was the importance of setting objectives in the lesson plan. The objectives should be based on what we have taught in the learning environment.
